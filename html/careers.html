Career Opportunities In ISRO

Software Developer

A software developer's role is to create and maintain software for systems and applications. They are responsible for the entire software development life cycle, from analyzing user needs to releasing the final product.

A Software Developer's role includes:

Software development:
Software developers at ISRO create software for systems that run the organization, including satellite control software, embedded systems, and ground-based applications.

Programming:
Software developers use a variety of programming languages, including C/C++, Python, Java, MATLAB, FORTRAN, Ruby, and LISP.

Computer networking:
Providing technical advice and guidance, and preparing technical documents and reports.

Testing and validating:
Ensuring the reliability and functionality of space systems through rigorous testing.

Project Manager

Project Manager:
A project manager at ISRO is responsible for ensuring that a project meets its deadlines, stays within budget, and adheres to quality standards. They manage timelines and resources to prevent miscommunication and scope creep.

A Project Manager's role includes:

Project level decisions:
Project managers make decisions at the project level through Project Executive Decisions, Project Management Decisions, and Project Reviews.

Project Director:
Each plan project has a Project Director who is the chief executive of the project.

Project/Programme Management Council (PMC):
This council monitors and reviews the progress of the project.

Project Management Office:
This office supports the decision-making process with data and analysis.

Data Scientist

Data Scientist:
A data scientist is an analytics professional who is responsible for collecting, analyzing and interpreting data to help drive decision-making in an organization.

A Data Scientist's role includes:

Analyze data:
Find patterns and trends in datasets to gain insights.

Create models:
Build algorithms and data models to forecast outcomes.

Understand the domain:
Understand the ecosystem and domain before defining and solving problems.

Have problem-solving skills:
In addition to mathematical skills, data scientists should have consultative and problem-solving skills.

Human Resources Officer

Research Scientist

Research Scientist
ISRO offers various opportunities for researchers to engage in R&D projects within ISRO as well as at various institutions engaged with ISRO for carrying out sponsored research projects.

A Research Scientist's role includes:

Research and development:
ISRO scientists conduct research and experiments in a variety of scientific fields to support India's space exploration efforts. This includes designing and developing technologies, satellite systems, and propulsion systems.

Project management:
ISRO scientists manage the research, development, application, and execution of projects and missions. This includes reporting to senior professionals and higher authorities, and guiding and managing junior team members.

Innovation:
ISRO scientists are at the forefront of innovation, conducting groundbreaking research and developing cutting-edge technologies.

Spacecraft Thermal Engineer

Space Thermal Engineer

A space thermal engineer at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) ensures that the temperature of a spacecraft's parts is maintained within a specified range to enable it to survive in space's extreme temperature variations. They use various sensors to monitor the temperature.

Thermal management is a critical aspect of spacecraft and launch vehicle design. It directly impacts the performance, reliability, and longevity of space missions.

ISRO and the Indian Institute of Technology Madras (IIT Madras) have partnered to establish a Centre of Excellence in Fluid and Thermal Sciences. The center will focus on research areas such as spacecraft thermal management and combustion instability in hybrid rockets.
