Research Areas

Planetary Science

Planetary science is the scientific study of planets, moons, comets, asteroids, and other celestial bodies in our solar system. It involves studying the composition, formation, and dynamics of these objects, as well as how they interact with each other.

Planetary scientists use a variety of methods to study the solar system, including:

Laboratory analysis:Studying the chemistry, mineralogy, and isotopic composition of samples from space.

Remote Observaton:Using cameras on spacecraft to observe planets, moons, and asteroids.

Rader:Using radar to determine the physical characteristics of asteroids and search for potentially hazardous ones.

Astrophysics

Astrophysics is the study of the universe and our place in it using the laws of physics and chemistry. It is a branch of space science that explores topics such as the birth, life, and death of stars, planets, galaxies, and nebulae. Astrophysicists use math and physics to research how the universe works and the life cycles of celestial bodies.

Some topics in astrophysics include:

Stellar astrophysics:
The study of stars and their interactions with other stars, supermassive black holes, and other perturbers.Stellar astrophysics is defined as the study of stars, their interactions with supermassive black holes, other stars, and various perturbers, focusing on stellar motions, relaxation of systems, and mass segregation within gravitational potential wells.

Theoretical astrophysics:
The study of physical cosmology. It uses mathematical models and templates to fit new results as they arise. Theoretical astrophysics involves several areas of physics.

Exoplanets:
One of the most rapidly developing fields of astrophysics.

Astrophysics is sometimes referred to as "star physics" because one of its major branches is the study of stars.

Satellite technology

Communication:
Satellites are self-contained systems that receive signals from Earth and retransmit them back. They are essential for online communication and information transfer, especially in remote areas.

Navigation:
Satellites provide geolocation and time information to GPS receivers. The Global Positioning System (GPS) is a global navigation satellite system (GNSS) that uses satellites in Medium Earth Orbit (MEO)

Internet access:
Satellite internet service is provided by constellations of artificial satellites in low Earth orbit (LEO). Newer satellites use the Ku band to achieve data speeds of up to 506 Mbit/s.

Indian Regional Navigation Satellite System:
Indian Regional Navigation Satellite System(IRNSS)provides location information in real-time, which can be used to calculate the best route to a destination.

Internet of Things (IoT):
Satellite IoT integrates satellite networks with IoT devices to enable data exchange and connectivity.

Space Propulsion

Spacecraft propulsion is any method used to accelerate spacecraft and artificial satellites. In-space propulsion exclusively deals with propulsion systems used in the vacuum of space and should not be confused with space launch or atmospheric entry.

Several methods of pragmatic spacecraft propulsion have been developed, each having its own drawbacks and advantages. Most satellites have simple reliable chemical thrusters (often monopropellant rockets) or resistojet rockets for orbital station-keeping, while a few use momentum wheels for attitude control. Russian and antecedent Soviet bloc satellites have used electric propulsion for decades,[not verified in body] and newer Western geo-orbiting spacecraft are starting to use them for north-south station-keeping and orbit raising. Interplanetary vehicles mostly use chemical rockets as well, although a few have used electric propulsion such as ion thrusters and Hall-effect thrusters. Various technologies need to support everything from small satellites and robotic deep space exploration to space stations and human missions to Mars.

Space Medicine

Space medicine is a medical specialty that focuses on the prevention and treatment of medical conditions that could impact space operations. It's a subspecialty of emergency medicine that evolved from aerospace medicine.

Space medicine includes:

Screening:
Identifying health issues before they become problems.

Healthcare delivery:
roviding medical care to space travelers.

Maintaining performance:
Ensuring that space travelers are able to perform their duties.

Preserving health:
Working to ensure the long-term health of space travelers.

Space medicine addresses medical issues that arise from the space environment, such as: Weightlessness, Radiation, Lack of a 24-hour day/night cycle, Muscle atrophy, and Bone demineralization.

Space Robotics

Space robots are a type of specialized robot that replaces humans in conducting scientific experiments, external vehicular activities, space exploration and other activities in space. Space robots are gradually changing the traditional modes of space transportation, on-orbit construction, on-orbit maintenance, and planetary exploration. It is one of the important enabling means for future unmanned and manned space missions.

The development of space robots presents design, fabrication, and control challenges, as such devices will operate in space where the environment differs greatly from Earth. The space robot field calls for collaborative efforts between physicists, chemists, biologists, computer scientists, engineers, and other specialists to work towards this common objective.

In an effort to disseminate the current advances in space robots, and to stimulate discussion on the future research directions in this field, Space: Science & Technology invites papers for a special issue in this area. This special issue is aimed at gathering the latest works by robotics researchers facing the diverse problems related to space robots.

Spacecraft Design

Spacecraft design is a process where systems engineering principles are systemically applied in order to construct complex vehicles for missions involving travel, operation or exploration in outer space. This design process produces the detailed design specifications, schematics, and plans for the spacecraft system, including comprehensive documentation outlining the spacecraft's architecture, subsystems, components, interfaces, and operational requirements, and potentially some prototype models or simulations, all of which taken together serve as the blueprint for manufacturing, assembly, integration, and testing of the spacecraft to ensure that it meets mission objectives and performance criteria.

Spacecraft design is conducted in several phases. Initially, a conceptual design is made to determine the feasibility and desirability of a new spacecraft system, showing that a credible design exists to carry out the mission. The conceptual design review ensures that the design meets the mission statement without any technical flaws while being internally consistent. Next, a preliminary design is carried out, where the focus is on functional performance, requirements definition, and interface definition at both subsystem and system levels.

The preliminary design review evaluates the adequacy of the preliminary design. In the following phase, detailed design is drawn and coded for the system as a whole and all the subsystems, and a critical design review is performed where it is evaluated whether the design is sufficiently detailed to fabricate, integrate, and test the system.

Microgravity Research

Microgravity research can lead to novel discoveries about everything from how molecules interact to how the human body adapts to spaceflight and can translate into benefits for healthcare, agriculture, industry, and much more, on Earth. Humanity has been performing scientific research in space for decade.

Microgravity is a unique environment where the effects of gravity are reduced or removed. This environment is created in freefall. In space, freefall is achieved by ‚Äúfalling around‚Äù the Earth in the International Space Station; on the Earth, freefall is achieved in special research aircraft that fly in parabola-like maneuvers. Anyone can experience freefall simply by jumping in the air: the moment your feet leave the ground, you are in freefall and experience exactly the same sensation as astronauts floating inside the space station! Microgravity is very important in the study of Space Science because the processes and phenomena normally hidden by dominant gravitational effects become more easily observable.

An example of a physical science experiment in freefall is the study of how a liquid occupies a partially filled container: on the ground, a liquid tends to pool at the bottom of its container, but in microgravity the liquid can behave strangely and may occupy both ends of its container with a void in the middle, creating new challenges in the design of spacecraft liquid fuel tanks and life support systems.

Lunar Research

Lunar research is the study of the Moon and its role in the solar system and Earth's history. Lunar research can help us understand the evolution of other planets, the composition of Earth's atmosphere, and the early history of Earth.

Here are some areas of lunar research:

Lunar surface:
Scientists can place instruments and sensors on the Moon to study surface-plume interactions, plasma interaction with dust, and the compression properties of the regolith.
Lunar eclipses:
Lunar eclipses can help us understand the Moon's soil structure and heat conductivity.
Moonquakes:
Studying moonquakes can help us understand what seismic activity on Earth was like during ice ages and Earth's early history.
Earth's atmosphere:
By measuring Earth's glow from the Moon, scientists can estimate the composition of Earth's atmosphere.
