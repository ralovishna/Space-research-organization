Space Visionaries Of India

Future Missions

NISAR(NASA-ISRO SAR Satellite)

NASA-ISRO SAR (NISAR) is a Low Earth Orbit (LEO) observatory being jointly developed by NASA and ISRO. NISAR will map the entire globe in 12 days and provide spatially and temporally consistent data for understanding changes in Earth's ecosystems

Expected launch date: March,2025

Mission Type: Earth Observation

Gaganyaan (Orbital Vehicle)

Gaganyaan is an Indian crewed orbital spacecraft (jointly made by ISRO and HAL) intended to be the basis of the Indian Human Spaceflight Programme. The spacecraft is being designed to carry three people,This will be the first of two flight tests prior to the inaugural of crewed mission.

Expected launch date: December, 2024

Mission Type: Human Spaceflight

Venus Orbiter Mission(Shukrayaan)

The Venus Orbiter Mission (VOM), unofficially known as Shukrayaan is a planned Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) mission to study the surface and atmosphere of Venus.

Expected launch date: 29 Mar,2028

Mission Type: Planetary Exploration

Lunar Polar Exploration Mission

(LUPEX), is a planned joint lunar mission by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency (JAXA). The mission would send an uncrewed lunar lander and rover to explore the south pole region of the Moon no earlier than 2028.

Expected launch date: March,2028

Mission Type: Lunar Exploration

Chandrayaan-4

Chandrayaan-4,is a planned lunar sample return mission of the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the fourth iteration in its Chandrayaan lunar exploration programme.The mission is currently under conceptualization.

Expected launch year: 2028

Mission Type: Lunar Exploration

Latest News

ISRO has successfully conducted the first uncrewed flight test for the Gaganyaan mission, marking a significant milestone in India's Human Spaceflight Program. The mission aims to demonstrate the performance of the crew module and escape systems, ensuring the safety of astronauts in future missions. This achievement showcases India's growing capabilities in space exploration and technology.

About Us

Welcome to the Space Research Organization, a pioneer in exploring the vast expanse of the cosmos and unlocking the mysteries of the universe. Since its inception, our organization has been at the forefront of advancing space science and technology, contributing significantly to humanity's understanding of space and its applications.

Our dedicated team of scientists, engineers, and visionaries work tirelessly to design and launch satellites, interplanetary probes, and space exploration missions that push the boundaries of human knowledge.
